Newton's Law of Cooling

Newton's Law of Cooling states that the rate of change of the temperature of an object is proportional to the difference between its own temperature and the ambient temperature (i.e. the temperature of its surroundings).

The differential equation equation will be: $$ \frac{dT}{dt}=-k(T-T_a)$$ Where;
T is the Temperature of a point at any given instant
t is the time
k is the Coefficient of proportionality
The Analytic solution of the differential equation is as follows:

Analytic Solution

Rewriting the differential equation as follows: $$ \frac{dT}{T-T_a} = -kdt$$ Integrating on both sides $$ \int \frac{dT}{T-T_a} = -k\int dt$$ $$ \log_{e}{T-T_a} = -kT + C$$ Taking Natural logarithm on both sides $$ T- T_a = Ce^{-kt}$$ $$ T = T_a + Ce^{-kt}$$ Thus, we have obtained solution of the differential equation, which tells us the value of T as every instant T.
