Solving First Order ODE


Series RL - Circuit

An inductor resists change in current, and eventhough we have a constant voltage source 'V', as soon as we connect the elements of the circuit the current will take some finite time to change. It is during this time interval that voltage accross the inductor opposes the current flow.

Consider for the given above image the voltage is constant

Applying Kirchoff Voltage Law

$$V_{DC} = iR + L\frac{di}{dt}$$ Rewriting the above using substitutions $$ \frac{di}{dt} = \frac{V_{DC} -iR}{L}$$

Thus, we now have the differential equation that describes the variation of current 'i' in the series RL-circuit.

For the sake of better understanding we will also solve the given equation analytically.

Analytic Solution

From the above differential equation, we can write: $$\frac{di}{V_{DC} - iR}= \frac{1}{L} dt$$ Integrating on both sides $$\int_{i_0}^{i} \frac{di}{V_{DC} - iR}= \frac{1}{L} \int_{0}^{t} dt$$ $$ -\frac{1}{R} log_e{\frac{V_{DC} - iR}{V_{DC}}}= -\frac{R}{L} t$$ Taking Natural logarithm on both sides $$ \frac{V_{DC} - iR}{V_{DC}} = e^{-\frac{R}{L}t} $$ $$ V_{DC} - iR= V_{DC}e^{-\frac{R}{L}t}$$ $$ V_{DC}(1-e^{-\frac{R}{L}t}) = iR$$ $$ i=\frac{V_{DC}}{R}(1-e^{-\frac{R}{L}t}) $$

Thus, we have the solution of the differential equation of the 'i'. The solution describes exactly how the current 'i' changes with respect to time.

Scilab Program

The entire program that can solve the given differential equation is 


			V0=5
			i0=0
			R = 50
			T0 = 0
			L = 1
			T = linspace(0,0.2,100);
			function di = f(T,i)
			    di = (V0-i*R)/L
			endfunction
			n = ode(i0,T0,T,f); 
			plot(T,n)
			xlabel("time(sec)")
			ylabel("Current(mA)")
			title("Solution of Series RC circuit")

R is the resistance

T is the array of elements representing moments of time
